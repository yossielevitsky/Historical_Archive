Hollandse Synagoge

בית הכנסת ההולנדי

The Hollandse Synagoge (Dutch Synagogue), officially known as the Synagogue Shomre Hadass, is one of the most significant and architecturally stunning religious landmarks in Antwerp.

Architectural grandeur

Built in 1893 by the architect Joseph Hertogs, the synagogue is a masterpiece of the Moorish Revival style. Its facade is imposing yet inviting, featuring intricate stone detailing and a grand central arch.

Historical Significance

For over a century, this synagogue has served as a central house of worship for the Shomre Hadass community. It has witnessed the fluctuating history of Antwerp's Jews, from the golden age of the late 19th century through the devastation of WWII and the subsequent rebuilding of the community.
