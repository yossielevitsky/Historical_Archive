Exploring the rich history, architecture, and lives of Jewish Antwerp.

Community Life

The last real shtetl in western Europe, Antwerp is known for its Orthodox Jews and its diamonds industry.

Historic District

Barely twenty years ago, approximately 80% of Antwerp’s Jewish population used to make a living from the diamond industry. More than half of the world production of diamonds passed through these few streets near Centraal Station. The diamond centers, which can be visited, also served as meeting places where ongoing debate took place over the social, cultural, and political issues affecting the Jewish community.
